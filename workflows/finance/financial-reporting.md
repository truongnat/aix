# Financial Reporting Workflow

## Overview
This workflow guides you through preparing financial statements, creating financial reports, and presenting financial data for stakeholders.

## When to Use
- Preparing monthly/quarterly/annual financial reports
- Creating financial statements for investors or lenders
- Analyzing financial performance
- Presenting financial data to management
- Regulatory reporting requirements

## Steps

### 1. Gather Source Data
- Collect all financial transactions for the period
- Reconcile bank accounts and credit cards
- Verify account balances
- Review and correct any errors
- Ensure all transactions are recorded

### 2. Prepare Trial Balance
- Sum all account balances
- Verify debits equal credits
- Identify and correct any imbalances
- Review for unusual account balances
- Ensure proper account classification

### 3. Prepare Financial Statements

#### Balance Sheet
- List all assets (current and non-current)
- List all liabilities (current and non-current)
- Calculate equity (assets - liabilities)
- Verify accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- Include comparative prior period data

#### Income Statement
- List all revenue streams
- List all expense categories
- Calculate gross profit (revenue - cost of goods sold)
- Calculate operating profit (gross profit - operating expenses)
- Calculate net income (operating profit +/- other income/expenses)
- Include comparative prior period data

#### Cash Flow Statement
- Calculate cash flows from operating activities
- Calculate cash flows from investing activities
- Calculate cash flows from financing activities
- Calculate net change in cash
- Verify with actual cash balance change

### 4. Calculate Key Ratios
- Liquidity ratios (current ratio, quick ratio, cash ratio)
- Profitability ratios (gross margin, operating margin, net margin)
- Solvency ratios (debt-to-equity, interest coverage)
- Efficiency ratios (asset turnover, inventory turnover)
- Compare with industry benchmarks

### 5. Create Financial Analysis
- Analyze revenue trends and growth
- Analyze expense trends and cost control
- Compare with budget or forecast
- Identify variances and causes
- Assess overall financial health

### 6. Prepare Executive Summary
- Highlight key financial metrics
- Summarize financial performance
- Identify significant changes from prior period
- Note any concerns or risks
- Provide recommendations

### 7. Create Visualizations
- Create charts for key metrics (revenue growth, expense breakdown)
- Create graphs for trend analysis
- Create dashboards for quick overview
- Use consistent formatting and colors
- Ensure visualizations are clear and accurate

### 8. Review and Validate
- Review all calculations for accuracy
- Verify data integrity
- Cross-check with source documents
- Review for completeness
- Get approval from appropriate stakeholders

### 9. Distribute Reports
- Determine distribution list (management, investors, lenders)
- Choose appropriate format (PDF, Excel, web dashboard)
- Ensure confidentiality and security
- Schedule regular reporting cadence
- Maintain report archive

### 10. Monitor and Improve
- Gather feedback on reports
- Identify areas for improvement
- Streamline reporting process
- Automate repetitive tasks
- Update report templates as needed

## Skills Used
- accounting-pro - For financial statement preparation and account reconciliation

- planning-pro - For creating reporting schedules and processes

## Success Criteria
- Financial statements are accurate and complete
- Reports are delivered on time
- Stakeholders understand the reports
- Key metrics are highlighted clearly
- Actionable insights are provided
- Reports comply with regulatory requirements

## Common Pitfalls
- Not reconciling accounts before reporting
- Missing transactions or incomplete data
- Calculation errors in ratios
- Not including comparative data
- Overcomplicating reports
- Not explaining variances
- Missing deadlines

```

  Cash and Cash Equivalents
  Accounts Receivable
  Inventory
  Prepaid Expenses
  Total Current Assets

Non-Current Assets
  Property, Plant & Equipment
  Intangible Assets
  Investments
  Total Non-Current Assets

Total Assets

LIABILITIES
Current Liabilities
  Accounts Payable
  Short-term Debt
  Accrued Expenses
  Total Current Liabilities

Non-Current Liabilities
  Long-term Debt
  Deferred Tax Liabilities
  Total Non-Current Liabilities

Total Liabilities

EQUITY
  Share Capital
  Retained Earnings
  Total Equity

Total Liabilities and Equity
```

  Depreciation

  Interest Income
  Interest Expense
  Net Other Income/Expenses

Net Income
