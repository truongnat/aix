# Financial Planning Workflow

## Overview
This workflow guides you through comprehensive financial planning including goal setting, budgeting, investment planning, risk management, and retirement planning.

## When to Use
- Creating a personal financial plan
- Business financial planning
- Retirement planning
- Major purchase planning
- Debt management planning

## Steps

### 1. Assess Current Financial Situation
- Gather all financial information (income, expenses, assets, liabilities)
- Calculate net worth (assets - liabilities)
- Analyze cash flow (income vs expenses)
- Review current insurance coverage
- Assess risk tolerance

### 2. Define Financial Goals
- Set short-term goals (1-3 years)
- Set medium-term goals (3-10 years)
- Set long-term goals (10+ years)
- Prioritize goals by importance and timeline
- Quantify each goal with specific amounts

### 3. Create Budget
- Track current spending patterns
- Categorize expenses (fixed vs variable, needs vs wants)
- Set spending limits for each category
- Include savings as a fixed expense
- Build emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)

### 4. Develop Investment Strategy
- Determine asset allocation based on risk tolerance
- Select appropriate investment vehicles
- Diversify investments across asset classes
- Consider tax implications
- Rebalance portfolio periodically

### 5. Manage Debt
- List all debts with interest rates
- Prioritize high-interest debt
- Create debt repayment plan
- Consider debt consolidation if beneficial
- Avoid new high-interest debt

### 6. Plan for Retirement
- Estimate retirement expenses
- Calculate retirement savings needed
- Maximize retirement account contributions
- Consider tax-advantaged accounts
- Review and adjust plan annually

### 7. Implement Risk Management
- Ensure adequate insurance coverage (health, life, disability)
- Create estate plan (will, power of attorney)
- Build emergency fund
- Diversify income sources
- Review and update regularly

### 8. Monitor and Adjust
- Review financial plan quarterly
- Track progress toward goals
- Adjust for life changes
- Rebalance investments as needed
- Update insurance coverage

## Skills Used
- financial-analysis-pro - For analyzing financial data and projections
- accounting-pro - For bookkeeping and financial statement preparation
- planning-pro - For creating structured plans

## Success Criteria
- Net worth increasing over time
- Emergency fund fully funded
- Debt decreasing or eliminated
- Investment portfolio aligned with goals
- Progress toward financial goals measurable
- Risk management adequate

## Common Pitfalls
- Not tracking expenses accurately
- Setting unrealistic goals
- Ignoring inflation in long-term planning
- Failing to adjust plan for life changes
- Taking on too much investment risk
- Not having adequate insurance
- Not updating plan regularly

## Next Steps
- Execute budget and track spending
- Implement investment strategy
- Set up automatic savings
- Review plan quarterly
- Adjust as needed based on progress

## Output Format

Financial plan document including:
- Current financial situation analysis
- Defined financial goals with timelines
- Budget breakdown
- Investment strategy and asset allocation
- Risk management plan
- Implementation timeline
- Progress tracking metrics
